## The hypothesis being tested

> Transformer LLMs achieve generative intelligence via dense matrix
> multiplication at massive scale. That math requires GPUs requires data
> centers requires trillion-dollar capital. Does there exist a fundamentally
> different mathematical foundation that can produce equivalent or superior
> generative intelligence without matmul at scale?

This is "Track B" of a larger question. Track A is "make matmul cheaper."
Track B is "replace it."

---

## What we have built

A four-layer symbolic reasoning architecture with grounded analogy and
multi-step planning, plus a parallel transfer-test domain. **Zero matrix
multiplication anywhere in the inference path.** Only XOR, integer
addition, sign, dot product over int8, dict lookups, integer increments.

## The headline finding (iterations 7 and 8)

**Grounded analogy is substrate-independent.**

HDC v3/v4 (bipolar 10000-dim vectors with role-weighted similarity) and
compression v5 (PPM-style integer counters over the same property table)
produce identical analog selections on every adversarial scenario in
both rule-world and traffic-world:

| Scenario | HDC | Compression | Match |
|---|---|---|---|
| rule-world S8 oil | wood (+0.515) | wood (count 2) | ✅ |
| rule-world S9 food | medicine (+0.498) | medicine (count 1) | ✅ |
| rule-world S10 medicine | food (+1.000) | food (count 1) | ✅ |
| rule-world S11 oil | wood (+0.515) | wood (count 2) | ✅ |
| traffic-world S6 horse_carriage | bicycle (+0.633) | bicycle (count 3) | ✅ |
| traffic-world S7 robotaxi | car (+1.000) | car (count 4) | ✅ |
| traffic-world S8 fire_engine | truck (+0.635) | truck (count 3) | ✅ |

This is the most important empirical result so far.

**What it means:** the right ingredient for grounded analogy is not the
specific math (HDC vs compression vs presumably anything reasonable). It is
the **property table + role weighting**. Two completely different
mathematical substrates converge on the same analog choices when given the
same grounded representation.

**Implication for the trillion-dollar question:** the question reframes.
The right question is not "what math primitive replaces matmul?" but "how
do we acquire the property table without authoring?" Every prior symbolic
AI program that hit a ceiling hit the property-acquisition ceiling, not a
math ceiling.

## What does NOT work (the honest list)

### Not generative in the LLM sense
- No natural language output mechanism exists. The system produces
  structured choices (action names, cited rule IDs, derivation chains).
  There is no path from those to fluent text without an LLM.
- Symbolic synthesis is bounded by token substitution. The system cannot
  invent a fundamentally new rule shape, only rename existing ones onto
  new objects.
- No mechanism for genuinely novel content beyond the substitution
  template.

### The parser does not transfer
- Regex patterns broke immediately on 5 of 8 traffic-world scenarios.
- Authoring traffic-world's parser took longer than authoring its rules.
- The parser is the place where the no-matmul hypothesis is hardest to
  defend. Closed-domain controlled input works; open-domain natural
  language probably needs a small classical NLP pipeline (not
  necessarily an LLM, but some neural component or massive grammar).

### The property table is hand-authored
- Authoring rule-world's table took ~10 minutes.
- Authoring traffic-world's table took ~15 minutes.
- For real domains the cost scales with vocabulary size. Scaling
  symbolically is exactly the bottleneck CYC hit.
- No mechanism currently extracts property candidates from rule
  predicate co-occurrence.
- No mechanism currently grows or refines the property table from
  observations.

### The role tags are hand-authored
- Same problem as the property table.
- No mechanism discovers role categories from rule structure.

### Other open issues
- Action sequences from the planner can include redundant no-ops because
  the planner doesn't penalize length when terminal scores tie.
- The parser-engine goal-predicate contract is fragile: the parser must
  guess what predicates the engine will produce.
- v4's projection relevance threshold is per-domain (rule-world 0.5,
  traffic-world 0.2). No principled way to set it.
- The substance vocabulary assumed single tokens until iteration 8
  surfaced multi-token names. There may be other hidden assumptions.

## Open research questions, ranked by importance

1. **Property table acquisition.** This is now the primary unsolved
   problem. Until it's solved, the architecture is bottlenecked on human
   authoring and the trillion-dollar question stays open.

2. **Parser robustness without LLMs.** Either build a classical NLP
   pipeline (dependency parser + entity linker + slot filler) and
   demonstrate it on multiple domains, or accept that controlled input
   is the right product mode for closed-domain reasoning.

3. **Genuine generative output.** No mechanism exists for producing
   fluent natural language from engine choices without an LLM. The math
   doc Part 5 listed compression-based prediction (PPM/CTW) over rule
   traces as the most underexplored matmul-free path. We have a tiny
   PPM-style baseline (`compression.py`) but it's used for analog
   selection, not generation.

4. **Validation that two transfer tests is enough.** A third domain
   (kitchen safety, first aid, wilderness survival) would either confirm
   the architecture is robust or surface more hidden assumptions like
   the multi-token substance issue from iteration 8.

5. **Action sequencing under richer goals.** The current planner finds
   sequences but uses goal predicates that the parser sets. A richer
   goal structure (multiple goals, goal preferences, soft goals) is
   needed for non-toy problems.

## What this experiment can claim

After 8 iterations and ~3000 lines of code:

1. **Closed-domain reasoning without matmul** is real, demonstrated, and
   transfers across domains.
2. **Grounded analogy** is real, demonstrated, substrate-independent,
   and transfers across domains.
3. **Autonomous rule and action synthesis** from grounded analogy is
   real and demonstrated end-to-end with behavioral closure in two
   different domains.
4. **The synthesis mechanism is bounded by token substitution** and
   cannot produce fundamentally new rule shapes.
5. **The parser layer does not transfer** and is the weakest link.
6. **Property table authoring is the labor cost** that limits scaling.
7. **Generation in the LLM sense is unattempted** and is the part that
   would actually challenge the trillion-dollar hypothesis.

## What this experiment cannot yet claim

- That it can reason about a domain where the property table has not
  been hand-authored.
- That it can produce fluent natural language output.
- That its synthesis mechanism scales beyond toy vocabularies.
- That it could replace a frontier LLM for any open-ended task.

The honest framing of the result so far: **we have built a credible
production reasoner for closed domains with hand-authored property
tables, with zero matmul, with full auditability, and with
demonstrated transfer between domains. We have not built and have not
attempted to build a replacement for general-purpose LLMs.**

The next experiments either close the gap toward general purpose
(option 1: property acquisition, option 3: a generative output layer)
or strengthen the closed-domain claim (option 4: a third transfer
test).
