# Where we stand — 2026-04-06 18:17 UTC
## North star (added 2026-04-07)

> **The goal is cheap AI, not matmul-free AI.** "No matmul" is a means,
> not the end. The end is driving the cost-to-build curve for capable
> AI down to where anyone could reproduce it on hardware they already
> own — the same trajectory every other technology in human history
> has eventually followed once someone actually tried to drive its
> floor down. Right now nobody is trying. The entire field is racing
> the ceiling up. That gap is the opening this project exists to
> exploit. Every iteration from here is measured against one question:
> *does this make the next person's version cheaper to reproduce?*
> Not "does it beat GPT-4 today." Not "does it generate Shakespeare."
> Does it move the floor down. Generative capability is on the road,
> but it is not the destination — the destination is the cost curve
> itself.

## The research question (unchanged)

> Transformer LLMs achieve generative intelligence via dense matrix
> multiplication at massive scale. That math requires GPUs requires
> data centers requires trillion-dollar capital. **Does there exist a
> fundamentally different mathematical foundation that can produce
> equivalent or superior generative intelligence without matmul at
> scale?**

This is not a question about optimizing transformers. It is a question
about whether the matmul → GPU → data-center pipeline is a mathematical
necessity or a contingent engineering choice.

After 14 iterations the question has been **partially reframed but not
answered**. The reframe: closed-domain *reasoning* and *grounded
analogy* and *structured explanation* are all clearly possible without
matmul. Open-domain *free generation* of arbitrary text remains
unattempted and is where the trillion-dollar moat may genuinely live.

## What is built (the architecture inventory)

A four-layer symbolic reasoning architecture with grounded analogy,
multi-step planning, structurally-induced property tables,
crystallization → induction loop closure, three-domain transfer test
coverage, and a verbose structured-to-text NL output layer with
honest bug reporting.

**Zero matrix multiplication anywhere in the inference path.** Only
XOR, integer addition, sign, dot product over int8, dict lookups,
integer increments, and string operations.

### Core architecture (rule-world, source of truth)
```

  engine.py              forward chaining + retraction + STRIPS planner
  rule_store.py          persistent JSON store with confidence + suppress
```

## What works, measured

### 1. Closed-domain reasoning at N=3 unrelated domains
| domain | rules | actions | scenarios | passing | v4 fires |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| rule-world    | 24 | 13 | 11 | 11/11 | scenario 11 (oil) |
| traffic-world | 14 | 11 | 8  | 8/8   | scenarios 6,7,8 |
| kitchen-world | 16 | 9  | 9  | 7/9   | scenarios 7,8,9 |

Two kitchen scenarios fail not because the architecture is wrong but
because of **two architectural bugs the third domain caught** — see
"Known bugs" below.

### 2. Substrate-independent grounded analogy (iteration 7)
HDC v3/v4 (10000-dim bipolar vectors with role-weighted similarity) and
compression v5 (PPM-style integer counters) produce **identical analog
selections on 7/7 adversarial scenarios across rule-world and
traffic-world**. The substrate doesn't matter; the property table does.

### 3. Property table induction at N=3 (iterations 9-12)
| domain | adversarial queries | top-1 agreement |
|---|---|---|
| rule-world    | 3 | 3/3 |
| traffic-world | 3 | 2/3 |
| kitchen-world | 3 | 3/3 |
| **combined**  | **9** | **8/9 = 89%** |

The single failure (traffic-world `fire_engine`) is a corpus-coverage
problem: `truck` appears in zero traffic-world rules, actions, or
scenarios. The inducer is silent because the corpus is silent.

### 4. Crystallization → induction loop closure (iteration 11)
Filtering the inducer's corpus to v4-only crystallizations (excluding
the symmetric pre-v4 syntactic ones) flips `oil → wood` from rank 5 to
rank 1. The loop runs end-to-end and contributes signal that improves
top-1 agreement on the headline metric.

### 5. Verbose NL explanations across N=3 domains (iteration 13)
**All 28 scenarios** in all three domains produce readable verbose
English explanations from a ~250-line template grammar over hand-authored
predicate dictionaries. Zero matmul. Zero learned model. The grease-fire
scenario in kitchen-world demonstrates honest bug reporting: the system
chose a bad action and the explanation says so explicitly with the rule
ID it violated.

### 6. Humanization dictionary induction (iteration 14, partial)
| domain | authored | induced | coverage |
|---|---|---|---|
| rule-world    | 44 | 17 | 38.6% |
| traffic-world | 37 | 8  | 21.6% |
| kitchen-world | 42 | 16 | 38.1% |
| **combined**  | **123** | **41** | **33.3%** |

Partial win. The bilingual trick reaches the humanization dictionary
much less tightly than it reached the property table. Honest finding,
not a failure.

## What does NOT work (the open problems, ranked)

### A. Open-domain free text generation
**This is the actual trillion-dollar question and we have not touched
it.** Everything iteration 13 produces is *explanation* of structured
reasoning the engine has already done. The system cannot generate a
poem, a story, a paragraph about a topic it has not been given rules
for, an answer to an arbitrary question, or any text whose content is
not pre-determined by the engine's deductive output. This is the part
that requires statistical learning, and it is the part the post-matmul
hypothesis would have to genuinely answer to count as a breakthrough.

### B. The parser layer does not transfer
Each of the three domains required hand-authoring its own regex/keyword
parser. Iteration 8 surfaced this as the largest remaining
"needs-human-authoring" surface and iteration 14 did not address it.
A general parser is the next major unknown.

### C. Scale (toy vocabularies only)
All three domains have ≤50 predicates and ≤10 substances. Real-world
domains have thousands of each. Nothing about the architecture
*prevents* scale, but it has not been tested at scale and we don't
know which assumptions will break first.

### D. Two known architectural bugs from iteration 12
1. **Multi-token v4 projection.** v4's analog selection handles
   multi-token substances (`raw_meat`, `fire_engine`) correctly, but
   the rule/action *projection* layer uses single-token substitution
   and silently fails. raw_egg in kitchen scenario 8 falls back to
   `do_nothing` because of this.
2. **Antecedent-removing visceral scoring.** `cover_grease_fire_with_lid`
   removes the `grease_fire` antecedent of R5, but the engine's
   `_score_state` evidently re-checks antecedents against the initial
   state and reports R5 as still violated. Kitchen is the first domain
   with this pattern. Both bugs are tractable; neither has been fixed
   to keep the architectural contract clean across iterations.

### E. The humanization dictionary is 67% hand-authored
After iteration 14, only ~33% of the predicate phrases are recoverable
by alignment. The structural fallback handles the rest readably, so
this is not blocking, but it is not the clean win iteration 11 was for
property tables.

### F. Property table is hand-authored for substances *not in the corpus*
Iteration 9-11 induce the table for substances that appear in rules
or scenarios. For genuinely novel substances introduced at runtime
(scenario-only tokens), induction has nothing to read. v4 still uses
the hand-authored table for these.

## What this experiment can claim, honestly

After 14 iterations and roughly 5000 lines of code:

1. **Closed-domain reasoning without matmul** is real, demonstrated
   end-to-end, and transfers across N=3 unrelated domains.
2. **Grounded analogy** is real, substrate-independent (HDC and
   compression both work), and transfers across N=3.
3. **Autonomous rule and action synthesis from grounded analogy** is
   real and demonstrated end-to-end with behavioral closure.
4. **Property-table induction from rule structure** reaches 89% top-1
   agreement at N=3 with no hand-authored property labels.
5. **Crystallization → induction loop closure** runs end-to-end and
   contributes the signal needed to flip a previously-failing query
   to passing on the adversarial set.
6. **Verbose fluent NL explanation** of every decision is generated
   for 28/28 scenarios across N=3 by template grammar over
   hand-authored predicate dictionaries.
7. **Honest bug reporting**: the system surfaces its own visceral-rule
   violations in the same paragraph as its decision. LLMs structurally
   cannot do this.

## What this experiment cannot yet claim

1. That it can produce arbitrary text. It cannot.
2. That it can answer open-ended questions. It cannot.
3. That its parser layer transfers. It does not.
4. That it scales beyond toy vocabularies. Untested.
5. That property and humanization induction approach 100%. They
   approach 89% and 33% respectively.
6. That it could replace a frontier LLM for any open-ended task. It
   could not, by design.
7. **That it answers the trillion-dollar question.** It does not.
   It answers a smaller question — closed-domain reasoning without
   matmul — convincingly. The big question remains open.

## The honest summary in one paragraph

We have built a credible, auditable, matmul-free reasoning + analogy +
explanation stack that works at N=3 domains, automatically acquires
~89% of its property knowledge from rule structure, automatically
acquires ~33% of its language vocabulary from rule statements,
explains every decision in fluent English with honest bug reporting,
and runs on a laptop in milliseconds. This is a real research result
about closed-domain reasoning. **It is not a breakthrough on the
trillion-dollar question, because we have not yet attempted the part
of generative intelligence that the trillion-dollar moat actually
guards: open-domain free text generation, statistical pattern
extraction from unstructured corpora, and acquisition of world
knowledge from text the system was not given as rules.** Iterations
15+ should be evaluated against that standard, not against any
"is this a usable product" standard.

## Open research directions ranked by closeness to the trillion-dollar question

1. **Open-domain text generation without matmul.** The hardest, the
   most valuable, and the one we have not started. Compression-based
   language modeling (PPM, CTW, arithmetic coding over byte streams)
   is the most promising matmul-free starting point, per
   `post-matmul-foundations.md` Part 2. Nobody has tried this with
   modern compute and modern data. The math doc names this as the
   single most underexplored direction.

2. **Statistical pattern extraction from unstructured text** without
   gradient descent on dense matrices. The Part 4 unsolved problem:
   "find a learning rule for distributed representations that is (a)
   gradient-free, (b) scales linearly in data, (c) provably converges
   to representations capturing the conditional structure of the
   training distribution, and (d) composes across layers." Hebbian
   + HDC is the obvious starting point. Untested at language scale.

3. **Acquiring rules themselves from text.** Right now rules are
   hand-authored. The whole architecture stands on a foundation of
   "a human writes rules in formal predicate form." If rules could
   be extracted from natural-language descriptions of a domain, the
   architecture would scale to domains nobody had to author by hand.
   This is the symbolic-AI dream and CYC's failure mode. Worth
   trying with modern compute.

4. **A general parser.** The largest remaining hand-authored surface
   inside the existing architecture. Probably needs *some* statistical
   component, but perhaps not a full LLM — classical NLP pipelines
   (tokenizer + dependency parser + entity linker) are matmul-light.

5. **Scale tests.** Run the existing architecture on a domain with
   hundreds of substances, hundreds of rules, thousands of scenarios.
   Find which assumption breaks first. Probably the retriever, then
   the planner.

6. **The two iteration-12 bugs.** Multi-token v4 projection and
   antecedent-removing visceral scoring. Tractable; both blocking
   one specific scenario each.

7. **A fourth and fifth transfer-test domain.** N=3 caught the bugs
   N=2 missed. N=5 might catch bugs N=3 missed. The diminishing-
   returns curve is unknown.

## What we are not doing

- Optimizing for any product use case.
- Pitching this as a tool, an MVP, or a startup.
- Claiming it replaces ChatGPT for any task.
- Hiding the open problems.
- Treating the closed-domain win as the answer to the open question.
- Stopping until we either answer the open question or conclusively
  prove it can't be answered with this approach.
